Knowledge

Knowledge is not the operation of subject but rather an affirmation of an idea in mind. It is not us who affirms something of a thing in our mind but rather it is the thing that affirms something of itself in our mind.

Knowledge is unfolding of an idea in the same sense as effect is an unfolding of cause.

VALENCES OF AN IDEA

  • Knowledge - affirmation of an idea in mind.
  • Consciousness - reduplication of an idea.
  • Affect - determination by an idea.

Kinds of Knowledge

First Kind of Knowledge

First kind of knowledge is defined by equivocal signs that can be divided into indicative signs that imply inadequate knowledge of things and imperative signs that involve inadequate knowledge of laws.

Second Kind of Knowledge

Second kind of knowledge is defined by common notions that is by shared composition of relations. It is an effort of reason to organize encounters among modes in accordance to the relations in which they agree with one another. From this kind of knowledge follow active affects that either surpass or replace the previous passive affects.

Common notions are not abstract ideas in the sense of abstracting certain aspects and reifying them as separate. They are general ideas insofar as they are based on shared relations or relations of agreement among multitudes and thereby lack knowledge of singular essences.

Third Kind of Knowledge

Third kind of knowledge is defined by direct knowledge of essences. This is no longer the knowledge through common compositions of relations but rather through the internal differences of intensity.
